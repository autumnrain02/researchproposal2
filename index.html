Lincoln Hall

Lincoln Preformance Hall Music Venue Information


The Lincoln Hall building at Portland State is the oldest building on campus. The venue is dedicated to the school of film, music, theater, and arts full of student performances. It was built in 1911, and the building underwent many renovations. Initially, the building was Lincoln High School but was later relocated. In 1952, the building served as the Portland State Extension Center Building. It is considered the first building to be used as classrooms on Portland State University's campus. In 1972, it became Lincoln Hall Performance Center.

Lincoln Hall's Music Venue

The preformance hall seats 465 people, where concerts are held. The concerts consist of students preforming in arts programs, where they have access to practice rooms, studios, and preform in the many auditoriums and theaters.
